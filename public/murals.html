Art as a Voice

I've always been a painter. Some of my murals exist simply for joy and beauty. Others carry a message. During COVID and the 2020 Black Lives Matter movement, I leaned into artivism, using murals as a voice for social justice across causes like Black Lives Matter, Stop AAPI Hate, Protect Our Elders, Indigenous Peoples' Day, and the "No Farmers, No Food" protest.

To me, art and design pull from the same well: understanding people well enough to move them. I love using visual craft to inspire and motivate people toward the world we want to live in.

My work has lived on walls across the Bay Area and beyond: a restaurant at Polk & Green in San Francisco, Mexicali Rose in Oakland, the Gilman venue in Berkeley (with a sister mural at the Dorchester Art Project in Boston), and a mural in Mexico City.
